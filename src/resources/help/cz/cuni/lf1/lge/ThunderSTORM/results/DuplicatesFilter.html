Removing duplicates

If two molecules are localized in the same image and their mutual distance is smaller than their localization uncertainty, then the molecule with lower localization uncertainty is kept and the other one is rejected. This post-processing method is suggested by Huang et. al. [1] to deal with repeated localizations that can occur due to overlapping fitting sub-regions in crowded-field data. ThunderSTORM allows users to specify the distance threshold as an expression combining mathematical functions and parameters from the table of results.
